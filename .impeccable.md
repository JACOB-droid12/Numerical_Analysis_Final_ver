## Design Context

### Users
The primary users are numerical analysis students and instructors working through assignment-style calculator tasks. They need to enter mathematical expressions efficiently, compare exact and finite-precision results, inspect chopping or rounding effects, and understand iterative numerical methods without needing a backend or setup process.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel clean academic, technical/calculator-like, and friendly like a patient student tutor. It should build confidence by making the computation path visible, labeling decisions clearly, and explaining why a method or setting is useful without sounding overly casual.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained educational workbench style: light-mode-first, clear typographic hierarchy, calm surfaces, precise controls, readable tables, and dark mode as a practical alternative rather than a neon theme. The visual language should preserve the current calculator/lab structure with modular panels, result summaries, and trace tables.

Avoid designs that are too playful, too dark or neon, too plain, or too corporate. Do not add decorative effects that compete with the math. Prefer subtle academic polish, crisp alignment, and strong readability over flashy motion or branding.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize mathematical clarity: every input, result, sign decision, tolerance, and iteration step should be easy to locate and interpret.
2. Preserve calculator efficiency: keep controls compact, keyboard-friendly, and fast to scan, especially for repeated assignment checking.
3. Teach while calculating: use short guidance notes and status messages to explain choices like exact versus machine signs without overwhelming the workflow.
4. Maintain accessibility and readability: favor high contrast, durable labels, large enough touch targets, responsive tables, and reduced-motion-friendly UI.
5. Keep visual polish restrained: use the existing token system, calm academic colors, consistent spacing, and purposeful emphasis rather than decorative or trendy effects.
